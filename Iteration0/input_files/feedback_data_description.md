The observed failures and ambiguities in the analysis are directly attributable to constraints described in the dataset:

1. **Finite Trajectory Length and Time Steps**: The Pomeau-Manneville (PM) map results show "fragile" tail fidelity and slow convergence to stable statistics. This is explained by the dataset description, which limits the PM map trajectories to 5001 steps. As noted in the results, deterministic intermittency requires significantly longer trajectories to populate extreme tails, leading to the observed pre-asymptotic artifacts and failure to capture the expected anomalous signature in the characteristic function.

2. **Spatiotemporal Coupling (Finite Velocity)**: The Lévy-Lorentz gas results show significant deviations from pure Lévy-stable behavior (e.g., variable tail index $\alpha$ across lags and ballistic fronts). This is directly attributable to the mechanism described: "ballistic transport... at constant speed v=1." This constraint imposes a finite-velocity cutoff ($\Delta x \le v \tau$) that masks the underlying power-law geometry at short lags, fundamentally distinguishing the Lévy-Lorentz gas as a "Lévy walk" rather than a "Lévy flight."

3. **Limited Observation Windows**: The CTRW datasets are limited to 500 time steps, whereas the PM map and Sisyphus cooling datasets contain 5001 steps. The analysis of tail index stability and characteristic functions is constrained by these varying temporal depths, which explains why some mechanisms (like the PM map) exhibit "fragility" or "pre-asymptotic effects" compared to the more robust CTRW models.
