**Title: Scaling Universality and Tail Fidelity in Anomalous Diffusion: A Multi-Scale Decomposition**

This study identifies the minimal effective theory for anomalous diffusion by mapping five distinct physical mechanisms onto a unified scaling framework, isolating structural "Lévy-ness" from scale-dependent drift. We will utilize Detrended Fluctuation Analysis (DFA) to extract robust Hurst exponents $H$ for each mechanism, effectively mitigating the high-variance artifacts inherent in raw MSD calculations across the finite-length trajectories. To quantify convergence toward the fractional Fokker-Planck limit, we will normalize the empirical probability densities $P(x,t)$ by their respective time-dependent scaling factors $t^{1/\alpha}$ and compute the Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence between these normalized distributions and the pure Lévy-stable reference. This approach isolates the shape of the distribution tails from the absolute scale, allowing for a rigorous comparison between the deterministic intermittency of Pomeau-Manneville maps and the momentum-dependent Tsallis statistics of Sisyphus cooling. By mapping the KL-divergence as a function of the tail index $\alpha$ and the DFA-derived scaling exponent $H$, we will delineate the universality classes of anomalous transport. This synthesis provides a statistically grounded criterion for determining which microscopic mechanisms are truly described by the half-Laplacian operator versus those requiring higher-order corrections or non-local effective dynamics.
