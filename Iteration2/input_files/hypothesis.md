The breakdown of the Fractional Fokker-Planck (FFP) approximation at the ultraviolet limit ($k > k_{break}$) is not merely a loss of resolution, but a signature of the "memory" of the microscopic mechanism. I hypothesize that the transition regime at $k \approx k_{break}$ contains a universal "crossover signature" in the phase of the characteristic function $\phi(k, t)$ that distinguishes the underlying physical mechanism (geometric scattering vs. deterministic trapping vs. velocity-dependent friction). 

Specifically, I propose to perform a **Complex-Phase Spectral Analysis** of the empirical characteristic functions. While the magnitude $|\phi(k, t)|$ provides the tail index $\alpha$, the phase $\theta(k, t) = \text{arg}(\phi(k, t))$ is sensitive to the symmetry and correlation structure of the microscopic displacements. I hypothesize that:
1. The Lévy-Lorentz gas (geometric) will exhibit a phase signature consistent with ballistic front propagation (linear phase shift).
2. The Sisyphus cooling (non-linear friction) will exhibit a phase signature consistent with state-dependent trapping (non-linear phase curvature).
3. By mapping the "Phase-Curvature Index" at $k_{break}$, we can construct a **Mechanism-Classification Map** that allows us to identify the physical origin of an unknown anomalous diffusion process solely from its spectral phase, even when the tail statistics (the magnitude) appear identical. This will move the field from "fitting distributions" to "identifying microscopic signatures in the spectral domain."
